1. PROBLEM STATEMENT
----------------------------------------
   Businesses spend billions on advertising without knowing which
   channels, segments, and campaign strategies drive sales. This
   project builds a machine learning model to predict sales from
   advertising spend, platform, customer segment, campaign score,
   seasonal factors, and other business variables — enabling data-
   driven budget allocation and revenue forecasting.

2. DATASET OVERVIEW
----------------------------------------
   Rows     : 2,000 synthetic campaign records
   Features : 16 raw  |  ~20 after engineering
   Target   : Sales (USD)
   Platforms: Social Media, Google Ads, Email, TV, Radio, Print
   Segments : Youth, Young Adults, Middle-Aged, Seniors, Corporate

3. PREPROCESSING SUMMARY
----------------------------------------
   - Removed duplicate rows (~1% of data)
   - Imputed missing values with column medians
   - IQR-clipped outliers in Ad_Spend and Sales
   - Label-encoded: Platform, Customer_Segment, Campaign_Type, Region
   - Engineered: Log_Ad_Spend, Log_Traffic, Spend_per_Channel,
     Score_x_Season, Quarter

5. KEY INSIGHTS
----------------------------------------
   • Ad Spend is the strongest predictor of sales (log-linear).
   • TV and Social Media yield the highest sales per dollar spent.
   • Seasonal Promo and Product Launch campaigns outperform others.
   • Corporate and Young Adult segments drive the highest revenue.
   • December sales are ~2× January — plan budgets accordingly.
   • Competitor index negatively impacts sales — monitor closely.
   • Combining 4-5 channels simultaneously boosts sales ~30%.

6. BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS
----------------------------------------
   1. Allocate ≥40% of ad budget to TV and Social Media.
   2. Run Seasonal Promo and Product Launch campaigns in Q4.
   3. Prioritize Corporate and Young Adult (26-35) segments.
   4. Use 3-5 marketing channels simultaneously for max reach.
   5. Boost ad spend 2-3× in Nov-Dec; reduce in Jan-Feb.
   6. Aim for CSAT > 4.0 — it measurably improves sales.
   7. Use this model to simulate spend scenarios before campaigns.
